Planning permission
Not necessarily. Many extensions can be built under Permitted Development (PD) rights, which means no formal planning application is required. PD rights allow single-storey rear extensions of up to 4 metres for detached houses (6m with prior approval) and 3 metres for semi-detached and terraced houses (under the Larger Home Extension scheme). However, PD rights can be restricted — for example if your home is in a Conservation Area, is a listed building, or if previous extensions have used up your allowance. We always check your PD position at the outset and advise accordingly.
The statutory determination period for a householder planning application is 8 weeks from the date of valid registration. For more complex full applications, it is 13 weeks. In practice, many applications are determined within this period, though some local authorities can take longer during busy periods. We will keep you updated throughout the process and follow up with the planning officer on your behalf after approximately 6 weeks.
If your application is refused, you have several options. You can appeal to the Planning Inspectorate within 12 weeks of the decision. You can revise the scheme to address the reasons for refusal and submit a new application (a revised application within 12 months of the original refusal is generally free of charge). You can also seek pre-application advice from the local authority on what would be acceptable. We will advise you on the most appropriate course of action based on the reasons for refusal.
A pre-application enquiry is a formal (and usually paid) request to the local planning authority for their view on a proposed scheme before a full application is submitted. It can be a useful way to test the acceptability of a design and identify any issues before committing to a full application. We can advise you on whether a pre-application enquiry is worthwhile for your project, and prepare and submit it on your behalf.
Extensions & loft conversions
This varies considerably depending on the type of extension, your location and the current value of your property. As a general guide, a well-designed extension can add between 10% and 20% to the value of a property in North London. Loft conversions that add a bedroom typically add around 15%. The key is that the extension is well-designed, properly built and adds genuinely usable space — a poorly designed or executed extension can actually reduce value.
Yes — semi-detached houses are very commonly extended. Single-storey rear extensions are often possible under Permitted Development. Side extensions on semi-detached properties generally require planning permission and are subject to restrictions on width. Double-storey extensions are also possible but almost always require planning permission. We will advise you on the specific options for your property during our initial consultation.
Many loft conversions can be carried out under Permitted Development, subject to certain conditions — for example the volume of additional roof space created must not exceed 50m3 for detached and semi-detached houses (40m3 for terraced houses). Dormers that face the highway always require planning permission. In Conservation Areas, any changes to the roof typically require planning permission. We will assess your specific situation and advise accordingly.
Working with us
Our fees vary depending on the type and complexity of the project. We provide a fixed-fee quotation after our initial consultation, which is always free of charge. Our quotation includes everything from the measured survey through to planning permission — with no hidden extras. Any council planning application fees are charged separately at cost. We are transparent about fees from the outset and will always discuss costs openly with you.
